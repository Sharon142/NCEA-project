Benjamin Franklin

Benjamin Franklin was one of the Founding Fathers and a polymath, inventor, scientist, printer, politician, freemason and he has a
diplomat. Franklin was one of those people who helped draft the Declaration of Independence and the US constitutes. Franklin negotiated
the Treaty of Paris in 1783 which helped end the Revolutionary War. His scientific investigations include electricity, maths, and mapmaking.
Franklin was a famous for being a clever writer. One of his famous works were "Poor Richard's Almanack".



Benjamin Franklin was born on January 17 1706 in colonial Boston. Franklin was the eight child out of the ten children his father Josiah Franklin
has in his second marriage. Franklin received very little formal education and his education ended when he was ten-years-old. This was not a problem
for Franklin because he was a bookworm and became a writer through self-taught.



In 1740s, Benjamin Franklin conducted experiments on electricity so that he could understand it further. Franklin invented the lightning rod that
protects buildings from fires produced by lightning. In 1752, he conducted a famous experiment which is flying a kite until a lightning struck the
kite and electrocuted him. Franklin formed a lot of electrical related terms like battery, charge and conductor.


Besides that, Benjamin Franklin studied ocean currents, meteorology, causes of common cold and refrigeration. Franklin invented a stove that more heat
requiring less fuel and bifocal glasses that have two purposes. Bifocal glasses are used for reading and allows the wearer to see people or things at a
distant clearly. In 1760s Franklin invented a musical instrument called "armonica". Wolfgang and Mozart wrote songs about it but in the 19th century the instrument
became useless.



Franklin died on April 17 1790, in Philadelphia at the age of 84. Franklin suffered from gout and complained about his food intake. Franklin gave Sarah Bache (his daughter),
most of the property in his will which he has written a year and a half before his death. Franklin donated money to schools, scholarships, and museum in Philadelphia and Boston.
Franklin shared his tombstone with his wife Deborah Franklin.
