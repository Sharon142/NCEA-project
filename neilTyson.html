Neil deGrasse Tyson

Neil deGrasse Tyson is an astrophysicist that made science famous through TV, radio broadcasts, and writing books. Tyson began interested in astronomy when he made a trip to
Hayden Planetarium at the American Museum of Natural History in New York City. In 1980, he received a bachelor’s degree in physics from Harvard University in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
Tyson obtained a master’s degree in astronomy from the University of Texas at Austin three years later. He started writing a Q&A column for the astronomy magazine StarDate.
This column later appear in the books which is Merlin's Tour of the Universe and Just Visiting the Planet.



Tyson took his first course on astronomy and astrophysics when he was eleven in Hayden Planetarium museum. When he was fourteen when he went to his astronomy camp in Arizona.
It was his first real trip away for New York. Tyson realized that the sky was approximate to the sky in Hayden Planetarium.



From 1995 to 2005, Tyson wrote monthly essays for "Natural History" magazine. Some of his essays were collected in Death by Black Hole: And Other Cosmic Quandaries which were
published in 2007. Tyson wrote an autobiography titled "The Sky is Not the Limit: Adventure of an Urban Astrophysicist". He published a book in 2017 titled "Astrophysicist for
People in a Hurry" and "Letters from an Astrophysicist" was published in 2019.



Being a director in the Hayden Planetarium, Tyson opened a new exhibit in categorized the solar system's bodies into groups. He created a controversial which is grouping Pluto in
the Kuiper belt objects instead of Jovian planets. Then, he received backlash which he responded through a book titled "The Pluto Files: The Rise and Fall of America's Favourite Planet"
in 2009.



Besides writing books, he made science a popular field through talk shows, television series, and a weekly radio show. Tyson appeared frequently on a talk show called "The Daily Show with
Jon Stewart" and "The Colbert Report". Tyson hosted a four-episode television series Origins examining the origins of stars, life, planet, and the universe. Tyson hosted television series NOVA,
scienceNOW, and, StarTalk, which is a weekly radio show.
